I'm an Econ and Applied Math student who got pulled into data science through research and never looked back. I've worked on protein function prediction in a computational biology lab and built statistical models for things like healthcare costs and opioid prescribing patterns. I've also TA'd courses in stats and econometrics on the side.
I'm more interested in why a model works than in just getting it to run. I like digging into the assumptions behind a method and figuring out where it breaks or gets misused, especially in fields like healthcare where bad statistics can actually hurt people.
Heading into a master's in data science next, with the goal of building enough depth in the math and stats underneath these models to use them responsibly and call out when others aren't.
